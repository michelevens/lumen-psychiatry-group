Getting Started

You meet with your provider via secure HD video from any device — phone, tablet, or computer. Appointments work just like an in-person visit: you discuss symptoms, treatment options, and care plans. Prescriptions are sent electronically to your preferred pharmacy. All you need is a private space and an internet connection.

Your first visit is a 60-minute comprehensive psychiatric evaluation. Your provider conducts a detailed clinical interview covering your current symptoms, medical history, family history, and treatment goals. You'll leave with initial clinical impressions and a personalized care plan. For some conditions like ADHD, a formal diagnosis may require additional follow-up visits.

Most new patients are seen within 3-5 business days. DPC (Direct Psychiatry Care) members get next-day availability. You can view open appointments and book directly through our online calendar — no phone call needed.

No. You do not need a referral to schedule with Lumen. You can book directly through our website. Some insurance plans (particularly HMOs) may require a referral for coverage — we can help you verify this before your first visit.

We are licensed and provide telehealth services in 30+ states across the United States. You can view our full licensing map on our homepage. If you relocate, your care continues as long as you move to a state where we're licensed.

Insurance & Pricing

We are in-network with Aetna, Blue Cross Blue Shield (BCBS), Cigna, UnitedHealthcare (UHC), Humana, Oscar, Ambetter, Medicare, Tricare, and Molina. We verify your benefits before your first visit at no charge. Compare insurance vs. DPC →

With insurance, you pay your standard copay per visit — typically $20-$50 for in-network mental health services. We handle all claims, billing, and prior authorizations. There are no additional fees.

Our Direct Psychiatry Care (DPC) plans start at $99/month and include visits, medication management, unlimited provider messaging, and priority scheduling. No surprise bills, no insurance paperwork, no hidden fees. View pricing →

DPC is a subscription-based model where you pay a flat monthly fee for access to psychiatric care — no insurance needed. Plans include office visits, medication management, unlimited messaging with your provider, and priority scheduling. It's ideal for those without insurance, with high deductibles, or who prefer a simpler payment model.

Medications

Yes. Our board-certified providers can prescribe psychiatric medications — including stimulants for ADHD — via telehealth in compliance with state and federal regulations. Prescriptions are sent electronically to your preferred pharmacy.

Yes, when clinically appropriate. Stimulant medications for ADHD and other controlled substances can be prescribed via telehealth in compliance with DEA and state regulations. Regular follow-up appointments are required for safe prescribing and monitoring.

Pharmacogenomic (PGx) testing analyzes your genes to predict how your body processes certain medications. It helps your provider choose the right medication — with fewer side effects and a higher chance of working. It's a simple at-home cheek swab — no blood draw or office visit required.
