### 1.1 Credentialing Acceleration (CRITICAL PATH)

The single highest-ROI action is unblocking credentialing. Every week of delay = weeks of lost EnnHealth direct revenue.

**Action Items:**

1. **Hire a credentialing service** — Do NOT do this yourself. Services cost $150-300/provider/payer and handle the entire process.

4. **State-by-state credentialing tracker** — Create a spreadsheet tracking:

#### Physical Location Requirements

**You do NOT need a physical office in every state.** Most states allow telehealth providers to practice from their home state into the patient's state with just a license. Exceptions:
- **Texas** — As of Jan 2026, HB 1052 requires a physical office in TX for telehealth coverage parity. A virtual office address ($50-100/mo) can satisfy this.
- **Some payers** may request a "service location" address during credentialing. Virtual office addresses work for this.

**Recommended setup:**
- **Florida:** Virtual office address ($75-150/mo) — for LLC, credentialing, Google Business Profile
- **Top 2-3 volume states:** Virtual office addresses ONLY if a specific payer requires a local service address ($50-100/mo each)
- **All other states:** Your FL address + state license is sufficient ($0)

#### Estimated Revenue by CPT Code

PMHNP rates are typically 85-100% of psychiatrist rates depending on payer and state.

| CPT Code | Description | Duration | Medicare Rate | Commercial Range |
|----------|-------------|----------|--------------|-----------------|
| **90792** | Psychiatric diagnostic eval (with medical) | 60 min | ~$175 | $150-280 |
| **99205** | New patient E/M (high complexity) | 60-74 min | ~$210 | $180-300 |
| **99214** | Established patient E/M (moderate) | 30-39 min | ~$110 | $85-160 |
| **99215** | Established patient E/M (high complexity) | 40-54 min | ~$150 | $120-220 |
| **90833** | Psychotherapy add-on (30 min) | +30 min | ~$65 | $50-90 |
| **99214 + 90833** | Follow-up + therapy combo | 45-60 min | ~$175 | $135-250 |

**Revenue optimization tip:** Always bill **99214 + 90833** instead of just 99214 when you do 16+ minutes of psychotherapy during a med management visit. This combo adds $50-90 per visit — on 30 patients/week that's an extra **$6,000-10,800/month**.

### 2.2 Contracting Strategy

#### Direct Insurance Contracts (via your own credentialing)

See **Section 1.1 Credentialing Hit List** above for the full Wave 1/2/3 payer priority list with reimbursement estimates and the Top 10 States matrix.

**Negotiation tips:**
- Start by accepting the standard rate to get paneled quickly
- After 6-12 months with volume, renegotiate rates (especially if you're in an underserved area or specialty)
- Always negotiate — payers expect it. Ask for 10-20% above the initial offer.
- Mental health has a provider shortage — you have leverage
- **BCBS is king** — they hold ~43% combined commercial market share and dominate 84% of metro areas. Prioritize the specific BCBS entity for each state you target.

#### EAP Expansion Contracts

Continue signing with EAP companies. Each one is a "set it and forget it" referral pipeline:
- You already have: Spring Health, Modern Health, Lucet/BCBS FL
- **Next targets:** Lyra Health, ComPsych, Magellan, Optum EAP, Carelon
- Apply to all simultaneously — each takes 30-60 days to activate

#### B2B / Facility Contracts (Higher Margin)

These are the contracts that really scale revenue:

1. **Substance Abuse / Detox Facilities** — Need psychiatric coverage for patients in treatment. Typically pay $150-300/patient/month or per-consultation fees. You already have the alcohol-use specialty page.

2. **Primary Care Practices** — PCPs want to refer out psych patients quickly. Offer a "same-week telehealth psych consult" partnership. The PCP refers → you see their patient → you send notes back.

3. **Correctional / Justice System** — Jails, prisons, and probation programs need psychiatric services. Telehealth makes this feasible at scale. Higher reimbursement, consistent volume.

4. **Skilled Nursing / Assisted Living Facilities** — Underserved market, telehealth psych for elderly patients. Medicare reimbursement.

5. **Colleges / Universities** — Campus mental health is overwhelmed. Offer overflow telehealth psych services. Contract with student health centers.

6. **Employer Direct Contracts** — Skip the EAP middleman. Approach mid-size employers (50-500 employees) directly: "Mental wellness benefit — your employees get same-week access to a psychiatric provider for $X/employee/month." Much higher margin than EAP per-session rates.

### 2.3 Partnership Strategy

#### Clinical Partnerships

1. **Therapist referral network** — Therapists can't prescribe. Build relationships with 20-30 therapists in your licensed states who will refer patients to you for medication management. This is THE most reliable organic referral source in psychiatry.
   - Join local (virtual) therapist networking groups
   - Offer "lunch and learn" presentations to therapy practices
   - Create a simple referral form on ennhealth.com
   - Send notes back to the referring therapist (they love this)

2. **PCP referral agreements** — Same concept. PCPs want to offload psych med management. Approach primary care practices in your credentialed states.

3. **Collaborative care model** — Some practices will pay you a monthly retainer to be their "embedded" psychiatric consultant. You review cases, adjust meds, see complex patients — all via telehealth. Reimbursable under CoCM CPT codes (99492, 99493, 99494).

#### Technology Partnerships

1. **EHR optimization** — If not using one, get a telehealth-optimized EHR:
   - **SimplePractice** — Popular for solo/small psych practices ($69-99/mo)
   - **Luminello** — Built specifically for psychiatry
   - **Valant** — Psych-specific with insurance billing built in
   - The right EHR automates: scheduling, reminders, billing, e-prescribe, notes

2. **Billing service** — Once volume exceeds 60-80 sessions/week, outsource billing:
   - Medical billing services cost 4-8% of collections
   - They handle: claim submission, denial management, patient billing, AR follow-up
   - ROI is almost always positive — they recover more than their fee in reduced denials

### 2.4 First Hire (Month 6-9)

When you're consistently at 80%+ capacity AND have a waitlist, it's time to hire.

**Option A: W-2 Employee PMHNP**
- Cost: $55-75/hr (varies by state/experience)
- You bill under your group NPI, keep the margin
- Full control over schedule, quality, processes
- Must handle payroll, benefits, compliance

**Option B: 1099 Independent Contractor PMHNP**
- Cost: Revenue split (typically 60/40 or 65/35, provider gets larger share)
- Less overhead and compliance burden
- Less control; they can leave anytime
- Must genuinely meet IRS independent contractor criteria

**Option C: Locum Tenens / PRN**
- Start with part-time coverage (evenings, weekends)
- Test providers before committing to full-time
- Agencies like Iris Telehealth, Talkiatry, or direct recruitment

**Hiring criteria:**
- PMHNP preferred (same license type = easier supervision model)
- Licensed in at least 5+ of your existing states
- Telehealth experience required
- Comfortable with ADHD + anxiety (your highest-volume conditions)
- Start part-time (20 hrs/week) and scale up
