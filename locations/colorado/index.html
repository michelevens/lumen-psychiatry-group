Mental Health Care Colorado Needs

Colorado is known for its stunning mountain landscapes, outdoor lifestyle, and booming tech economy — but behind the postcard scenery lies a mental health crisis that demands urgent attention. Colorado consistently ranks among the top 10 states in the nation for suicide rates, and the state's rapid population growth has far outpaced the expansion of its mental health infrastructure.

The Denver-Boulder metro area has surged with new residents, driving demand for psychiatric care to record levels. Wait times for a new-patient psychiatric evaluation in the Front Range can stretch 6 to 12 weeks or longer. Meanwhile, mountain communities like Aspen, Vail, Steamboat Springs, and Durango face extreme provider shortages, with some counties having zero practicing psychiatric providers. Lumen bridges that gap by delivering board-certified telehealth psychiatry to every corner of the state — no mountain pass required.

Altitude & Mental Health

Peer-reviewed research has linked higher altitudes to increased rates of depression and suicidal ideation, possibly due to reduced oxygen affecting brain serotonin pathways. Lumen providers are trained to consider altitude-related factors when developing treatment plans and selecting medications for Colorado patients.
