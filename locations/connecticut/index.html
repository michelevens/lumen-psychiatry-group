Psychiatric Care in Connecticut

Connecticut is a state of stark contrasts when it comes to mental health. Fairfield County suburbs like Greenwich, Darien, and Westport rank among the wealthiest communities in America, yet just miles away, cities like Bridgeport, Hartford, and New Haven face severe mental health disparities, provider shortages, and limited access to psychiatric care. Across the state, the pressure of high cost of living, competitive academic environments, and demanding careers drives significant rates of anxiety, depression, and burnout.

A large portion of Connecticut's workforce commutes to New York City — spending hours each day on Metro-North or I-95 — leaving little time for traditional in-office appointments. Lumen Psychiatry Group solves this with telehealth psychiatric care that fits around your schedule. Whether you're a Stamford finance professional, a New Haven graduate student, or a Hartford family navigating mental health challenges, our board-certified providers deliver expert care directly to your screen from anywhere in Connecticut.

Connecticut Mental Health Parity

Connecticut has some of the strongest mental health parity protections in the nation. State law mandates that all fully insured health plans cover mental health services at the same level as medical and surgical benefits — including deductibles, copays, and visit limits. This applies to telehealth psychiatry appointments as well.
