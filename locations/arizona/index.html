Closing Arizona's Mental Health Gap

Arizona is one of the fastest-growing states in the nation, yet it consistently ranks among the lowest for mental health provider availability. The explosive population growth in Maricopa County — home to Phoenix, Scottsdale, Mesa, Chandler, and Tempe — has far outstripped the expansion of psychiatric infrastructure, leaving millions of residents competing for a limited number of providers with wait times that can stretch months.

Beyond the metro areas, the challenge intensifies. Arizona's vast geography includes tribal lands, rural desert communities, and border towns where the nearest psychiatric provider may be hours away. Large Native American communities face some of the most severe mental health disparities in the country, with rates of depression, substance use, and suicide that far exceed national averages. Meanwhile, Arizona's substantial retiree population in communities like Sun City, Green Valley, and Sedona confronts unique barriers — mobility limitations, complex medication regimens, and late-life psychiatric conditions that demand specialized expertise.

Lumen was built to bridge these gaps. Our telehealth platform delivers board-certified psychiatric care to every corner of Arizona — no long drives, no months-long waits, no barriers between you and the care you need.

Heat & Mental Health

Research published in JAMA Psychiatry and other journals links extreme heat to increased psychiatric emergency visits, worsened mood disorders, and higher rates of aggression. Arizona's intense summers can exacerbate existing conditions and create barriers to in-person care. Lumen's telehealth model ensures uninterrupted access to psychiatric care from the comfort of your air-conditioned home, even during the hottest months.
