About This Screening Tool

The Generalized Anxiety Disorder 7-item scale (GAD-7) is a validated screening tool developed by Drs. Spitzer, Kroenke, Williams, and Lowe. It is one of the most widely used anxiety screening instruments in clinical practice, measuring the severity of generalized anxiety symptoms over the past two weeks.

No. This quiz is a screening tool, not a diagnostic instrument. Only a licensed healthcare provider can diagnose an anxiety disorder through a comprehensive clinical evaluation. Many medical conditions — thyroid disorders, cardiac issues, medication side effects — can mimic anxiety symptoms.

The GAD-7 total score ranges from 0 to 21. Scores of 0-4 indicate minimal anxiety, 5-9 mild anxiety, 10-14 moderate anxiety, and 15-21 severe anxiety. A score of 10 or higher is commonly used as a clinical cutoff suggesting further evaluation is warranted.

If your score suggests moderate or severe anxiety, consider scheduling an evaluation with a licensed mental health provider. Anxiety disorders are among the most treatable mental health conditions. At Lumen, we offer telehealth evaluations in 30+ states. Book your evaluation here.
